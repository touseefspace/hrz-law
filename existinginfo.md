## If You Have a Case, Why Consult Your Friend?

Studies show that many individuals consult friends or relatives instead of lawyers, which may lead to incorrect legal decisions. This happens for several reasons:

1. The belief that friends and family care more about their interests than legal advisors.
2. Fear of high consultation fees.
3. Negative shared experiences from others.

To address this concern, the firm focuses on building strong trust with clients through transparency and clarity from the very first consultation.

The firm also works to reduce financial burdens by offering **free legal advice** in several case types, including:

Commercial cases
Rental disputes
Civil cases
Financial claims
Criminal cases
Drug-related cases
Contracts and agreements

Free consultations are available via phone and remote services.

## Divorce

Divorce refers to the legal separation of spouses through formal procedures recognized by Islamic jurisprudence and applicable legal systems.

There are several classifications of divorce, including:

Revocable divorce
Irrevocable minor divorce
Irrevocable major divorce
Khula (mutual separation with compensation)
Judicial divorce

Each type follows specific legal conditions depending on circumstances and applicable law.

## Legal Consultation – Why Is It Important?

Legal consultation protects individuals from problems before they arise. It helps secure your interests, family, and finances.

Many people avoid legal consultation due to concerns about fees. However, proper consultation may prevent major financial losses and future legal complications.

Professional legal advice ensures stability and supports individuals and businesses in making informed decisions regarding contracts, agreements, and investments.
